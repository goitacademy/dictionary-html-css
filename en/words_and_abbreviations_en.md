# Words and abbreviations in CSS classes

The division into subsections is conditional and does not constitute an obligation.

Done in addition to <https://github.com/goitacademy/dictionary-html-css/blob/main/uk/common-words.md>.

## Large blocks/elements

### `page`

Page (usually the root element). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `page`

### `container`

Container, wrapping. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `container`

### `grid`

Grid (for the layout of blocks on a modular grid). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `grid` (element inside which there will be a modular grid)

### `row`

Wrapping for the cells of a modular grid. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `grid__row` (one or more "rows" of a modular grid)

### `column`

Modular grid cell. <br> Abbreviation: `col`  <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-md-6` (cell occupying 6 columns on the MD width)

### `wrapper`

The wrapping of something. <br> Abbreviation: `wrap`  <br> Example: <br> `contacts__wrapper` (wrapping around the content of the contact block)

### `inner`

The inner wrapping of something (usually to limit the width and center alignment). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `promo__inner` (inner wrapping of the promotional block)

### `navigation`

Navigation. <br> Abbreviation: `nav`  <br> Example: <br> `main-nav` (main navigation)

### `promo`

Internal advertising, some kind of introduction. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `promo` <br>

### `features`

Benefits, advantages. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `features` (a block with a description of properties and benefits)

### `cart`

The shopping cart. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `cart` (cart block on the cart page)

### `sidebar`, `aside`

Additional information (often a narrow column on a blog page). <br> Abbreviation: none  <br> Example: `sidebar`, `aside`  <br>

## Small blocks/elements

### `item`, `element`, `part`

A part, an element, a separate "unit". A universal word if you can't come up with something specific. <br> Abbreviation: `element` → `el`  <br> Example: <br> `main-nav__list-item` (item in the list in the main navigation, probably, `li`), <br> `filter__part` (a typical part of the filter)

### `widget`

Widget <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `aside__widget` (widget in the additional information block)

### `logo`

Logo. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `logo` (You won't believe it...) `.partner__logo` (partner logo)

### `field`

Form field. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `field-text` (universal block for setting forms), <br> `field-radio` (universal block for setting forms), <br> `field-checkbox` (universal block for setting forms), <br> `field-select` (universal block for setting forms), <br> `field-file` (universal block for setting forms)

### `pagination`

Multiple page navigation (1 2 3 ...). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `pagination` <br>

### `breadcrumbs`

"Breadcrumbs" (Home > Catalog > Product). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `breadcrumbs` <br>

### `modal`

Modal window. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `modal` <br>

### `popup`

A pop-up block (usually when you click on something). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `popup` <br>

### `tooltip`

A tooltip, a small block (usually by hovering over something). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `tooltip` <br>

### `copyright`

Copyright. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `copyright` <br>

### `button`

Button/buttons. The button itself is always a [BEM block](http://nicothin.github.io/idiomatic-pre-CSS/#bem-block). It can be mixed with BEM elements. The word can be used in wrapper classes (see example). <br> Abbreviation: `btn` <br> Example: <br> `btn` (separate button), <br> `.promo__btns` (wrap for buttons in the promo box)

### `title`, `heading`, `caption`

Header (often used on heading tags, but not necessarily on them). <br> Abbreviation: none  <br> Example: <br> `promo__title` (promo block header)

### `subtitle`

Subtitle. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `post__subtitle` (blog entry subtitle)

### `name`

Name. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: `product__name` <br>

### `slogan`

Slogan. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `logo__slogan` (slogan in the logo block)

### `user`

User. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `user` (block of the authorized/unauthorized visitor, for example, in the header)

### `label`, `tag`

Label, tag. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `label` (label), <br> `post__tags-list` (a list of blog entry tags)

### `category`

Categories. <br> Abbreviation: `cat`  <br> Example: <br> `post__cats` (blog entry categories)

### `dropdown`

A drop-down item (the direction of the drop-down does not matter). <br> Abbreviation: `drop`  <br> Example: <br> `filter__drop` (drop-down block on click in the filter)

### `search`

Search. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `search-form` (a quick search box, perhaps in the "header")

### `socials`

Social media (icons, generally). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `socials__list` (list with social links)

### `carousel`, `slider`

Carousel, slider (several consecutive slides). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `promo__slider` (carousel in the promo block)

### `header`

The top part, "header". <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `page-header` (page "header"), <br> `post__header` (blog post "header")

### `footer`

The lower part, "footer" <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `page-footer` ("footer" of the page), <br> `post__footer` ("footer" of the blog entry)

### `additional`

The addition of something. <br> Abbreviation: `add`  <br> Example: <br> `location__btn-add` ("Add button" in a block somewhere)

### `info`, `meta`

Information about an entity. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Пример: <br> `post__info` (blog post information)

### `body`

The main content part of something. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `post__body` (the main text fragment of a blog entry)

### `content`

The contents of anything. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `post__content` (the main text fragment of a blog entry)

### `section`

A large section of anything. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `promo__section` (large promo block section)

### `icon`

Icon. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `icon--twitter` (social network icon (modifier))

### `link`

Link. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `product__more-link` (the "Next" link at a product block)

### `list`

List. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `features__list` (list of features)

### `description`

Description of an entity <br> Abbreviation: `descr` <br> Example: <br> `product__descr` (product description)

### `image`

Image. <br> Abbreviation: `img`  <br> Example: <br> `promo__img` (promotional block image)

### `picture`

Image. <br> Abbreviation: `pict`, `pic`  <br> Example: <br> `promo__pict` (picture in the promo box)

### `toggle`

Switch, tumbler. Often a "hamburger" that switches the display/hiding of the main menu (on a media condition, where the menu is hidden under the "hamburger"). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `main-nav__toggle` (Main menu visibility switch)

### `tab`

Tab. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `tabs` (tabs), <br> `promo__tab` (tab in the promotional block)

### `thumbnail`

Thumbnail (usually in a gallery of any kind). <br> Abbreviation: `thumb` <br> Example: <br> `photo__thumb` (thumbnail in a photo gallery)

### `avatar`

Avatar, a small picture of the user. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `user__avatar` (user avatar, in the user block in the header)

### `text`

A text fragment (usually taken from the CMS). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `about__text` (The "about us" text is probably a wrap around multiple paragraphs)

### `author`

Author. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `post__author` (name/nickname of the author of the blog entry)

### `more`

"continue", "more". <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `product__more-link` (a button or link in the promotional block, offering more details)

## Modifications, states

### `active`, `current`

Active element (navigation point, active tab). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `main-nav__item--active` (modifier on the main navigation item, corresponding to the page the visitor is on)

### `hidden`

Hidden. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `product--hidden` (modifier that hides the product block)

### `shown`

Shown. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `product--shown` (modifier showing the product)

### `error`

Error status. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `field-text--error` (modifier that marks a block of a text field as incorrect)

### `success`

State of success. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `field-text--success`

### `warning`

Warning. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `btn--warning` (modifier that changes the appearance of a button)

### `pending`

Waiting for something (e.g., a response from the server after an asynchronous form submission). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `btn--pending` (modifier that changes the appearance of the button)

## Dimensions

### `extra small`

Extra small. <br> Abbreviation: `xs` <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-xs-6` (cell occupying 6 columns of the modular grid on the corresponding width), <br> `screen-xs` (variable name with the corresponding viewport width in the value)

### `small`

Small. <br> Abbreviation: `sm` <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-sm-6` (cell occupying 6 columns of the modular grid on the corresponding width), <br> `screen-sm` (variable name with the corresponding viewport width in the value)

### `medium`

Medium. <br> Abbreviation: `md` <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-md-6` (cell occupying 6 columns of the modular grid on the corresponding width),  <br> `screen-md` (variable name with the corresponding viewport width in the value)

### `large`

Large. <br> Abbreviation: `lg` <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-lg-6` (cell occupying 6 columns of the modular grid on the corresponding width), <br> `screen-lg` (variable name with the corresponding viewport width in the value)

### `extra large`

Extra large. <br> Abbreviation: `xl` <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-xl-6` (cell occupying 6 columns of the modular grid on the corresponding width), <br> `screen-xl` (variable name with the corresponding viewport width in the value)

### `extra extra large`

Huge. <br> Abbreviation: `xxl` <br> Example: <br> `grid__col-xxl-6` (cell occupying 6 columns of the modular grid on the corresponding width), <br> `screen-xxl` (variable name with the corresponding viewport width in the value)

### `narrow`

Narrow. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `btn--narrow` (modifier that makes the button narrow)

### `wide`

Wide. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `btn--wide` (modifier that makes the button wide)

### `phone`, `mobile`

Mobile devices (phones up to 5 inches). <br> Abbreviation: none  <br> Example: <br> `promo--phone` (modification of the promo block for the corresponding devices)

### `tablet`

Tablets (approximately up to 12 inches). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `promo--tablet` (modification of the promo block for the corresponding devices)

### `notebook`, `laptop`

Notebooks. <br> Abbreviation: none  <br> Example: <br> `promo--laptop` (modification of the promo block for the corresponding devices)

### `desktop`

Desktop Computers. <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `promo--desktop` (modification of the promo block for the corresponding devices)

## Other

### `previous`

Previous (often a link/button). <br> Abbreviation: `prev`  <br> Example: <br> `slider__prev` (button/arrow on the slider)

### `next`

Next (often a link/button). <br> Abbreviation: none <br> Example: <br> `slider__next` (button/arrow on the slider)

### `advertisement`

Advertising fragment (careful, it can be removed by ad blockers). <br> Abbreviation: `adv`  <br> Example: <br> `aside__adv` (an advertising block in the sidebar)

### `background`

Background (it's often some kind of change in the background color of a block). <br> Abbreviation: `bg` <br> Example: <br> `top-rated--bg-gray` (modification of the background color for the block of the highest rated products/services)

### `number`

Number. <br> Abbreviation: `num`  <br> Example: <br> `field-text--num` (modifier for the text field, which allows to design the numeric fields in a different way)
